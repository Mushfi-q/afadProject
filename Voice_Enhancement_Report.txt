  * Spectral Flatness (Vocoder detection)
  * Zero Crossing Rate (ZCR) Variance (Temporal complexity)

Stage 3: Weighted Scoring Implementation
- Moved away from "Hard Thresholds" (If X > Y then Fake) which caused false 
  positives.
- Implemented a points-based system where different features contribute to a 
  final "Fake Score".
- Added "Negative Cues" (e.g., high Rolloff indicates Natural speech, subtracts points).

3. KEY FINDINGS
---------------
- High Spectral Flatness (> 0.08) is the most reliable indicator of noise and 
  artifacts introduced by neural vocoders (MelGAN, HiFi-GAN).
- Real Human Speech is dynamic and unpredictable. Low ZCR Variance (< 0.007) 
  strongly suggests the regular, mathematical stability of AI generators.
- Spectral Rolloff is a "Truth Marker". High-quality real recordings have 
  significant energy in higher bands (Rolloff > 3600), whereas many TTS 
  systems roll off much earlier.
